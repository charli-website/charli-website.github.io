Consultations with Humanitarian Practitioners

The National Science Foundation's Human and Social Dynamics Initiative has provided exploratory funding for the collection of focus group data to assess the existing state of knowledge within the international humanitarian community regarding this category of war-affected child, as a prelude to designing a more comprehensive study.

These focus groups were scheduled for December 2004, February 2005 and March 2005, at Columbia University's Mailman School of Public Health, University of Geneva's Graduate School for International Studies, and University of Pittsburgh's Graduate School of Public and International Affairs. Participants were invited from human rights and humanitarian organizations with programming in gender-based violence and/or child protection in conflict zones.

The focus groups consisted of a semi-structured conversation among these practitioners regarding general issues with respect to the protection of war-affected children and women, as well as specific questions about how to protect survivors of wartime sexual violence and children brought to term as a result. Participants were asked for their perspectives on how best to design a global study evaluating the protection needs of this population.

The results will be disseminated as an issue paper identifying the key points of consensus and of debate in the focus groups regarding best practices and research priorities. Free copies of the issue paper will be provided to all participating organizations by Fall 2005 and a version of the paper will be submitted for publication through the Humanitarian Practice Network.
