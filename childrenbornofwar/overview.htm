The "Children as Legacies of War" research initiative at University of Pittsburgh encompasses several projects. In addition to a sole-authored book-in-progress on the human rights of "children born of war," the project includes work toward an interdisciplinary edited volume exploring this issue from different theoretical perspectives and in different country contexts. It also involves a series of focus groups with humanitarian practitioners to assess existing knowledge and practice with respect to protecting this population in war-affected areas, and to consult practitioners on how to design a comprehensive global study. The institutional home for the research initiative is the Ford Institute for Human Security at University of Pittsburgh's Graduate School of Public and International Affairs. Funders supporting this project include the John D. And Catherine T. MacArthur Foundation, the International Studies Association, and the National Science Foundation.
